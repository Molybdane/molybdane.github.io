Spotlight

Public stamp mills

Public stamp mills are exactly what they sound like, publicly accessible stamp mills that you can drive up to have your rocks crushed. As such, they can help alleviate crushed rock shortages. They might also provide a leg up for any starting entrepeneur or aspiring miner. We at the Loudmouth know of two examples of these:

Juanrock stampmill

Just west of government store 1 at (coordinates), this stamp mill is available for 0.1 L to crush 20 rocks. The crushed stone must be picked up at the stamp mill itself, and labour must be provided. It is perfect for getting to grips with crushing smaller amounts of rock that doesn't warrant the investment of a stamp mill.

The public limestone mine stamp mill

The public limestone mine at (coordinates) south of government store 1 has 2 stamp mills available to immediately crush your earnings. The use of these stamp mills is free of charge, but labour must still be provided.

Wrapping up

Publicly accessible machinery like this might be what is necessary to get the economy back on track, either directly or by example; what other machinery can be turned into a profit in a similar manner, if labour is already provided?
